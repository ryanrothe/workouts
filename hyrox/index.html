How the 12 weeks are built

A periodized build toward Hyrox-style fitness using your home gym, with every run swapped for low-impact cardio so your hips stay happy. Three phases, four sessions a week, 45–60 minutes each.

1
Base · Weeks 1–4. Aerobic base, movement quality, hip prep, strength-endurance foundation. Wk 4 = first Half-Hyrox benchmark.
2
Build · Weeks 5–8. Threshold engine, sled-substitute power, heavier strength-endurance. Wk 8 = repeat the Half-Hyrox and chase your baseline.
3
Peak · Weeks 9–12. Full simulations, race-pace pacing & transitions, then taper. Wk 11 peak sim, Wk 12 final race-day sim.

The four-day week

Day A

Lower strength + a sled-substitute / engine primer.

Day B

Low-impact engine — Zone 2 in Base, threshold & race-pace later.

Day C

Upper strength + pull (sled-pull pattern) + wall-ball volume.

Day D

The long day — compromised cardio & Hyrox simulations.

Why no running (and when you do run)

The 8×1 km runs are replaced with Airbike and rowing efforts — same "compromised running" cardio stimulus, none of the pounding your hips don't love. Running stays as a weekend thing on the basketball court, not a programmed weekday load. If you ever want a run, the treadmill incline power-walk is the only foot-strike option built in, and it's always optional.

Want a shorter block? Stop at Week 8 (run Phases 1–2, treat the Wk 8 benchmark as your finish line) for an 8-week version, or add the first two Peak weeks for a 10-week version.

How to load

  • RPE 7 = ~3 reps left. Your default strength working zone early on.
  • RPE 8 = ~2 reps left. Build-phase strength target.
  • Add ~2.5–5% load when all prescribed reps are clean at the target tempo.
  • Station weight goals = Hyrox Men's Open: farmers carry 2×24 kg, sandbag 20 kg, wall ball 6 kg / 100 reps.
